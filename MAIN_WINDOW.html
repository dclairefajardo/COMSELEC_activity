BENEFITS OF BEING A USG OFFICERS

Leadership Experience:
Serving in a student government position allows individuals to develop and enhance their leadership skills. It provides practical experience in decision-making, communication, and teamwork.

Representation:
Student leaders often represent the student body in discussions with university administrators, faculty, and other stakeholders. This role allows students to voice their concerns and advocate for their needs.

Networking Opportunities:
Engaging in student government activities provides opportunities to network with fellow students, faculty, and even professionals outside the university. This can be valuable for future career opportunities.

Personal Development:
Being part of a student government helps individuals grow personally and professionally. It can improve public speaking skills, boost confidence, and foster a sense of responsibility.

Community Service:
Many student governments are involved in community service and outreach programs. Engaging in such activities allows students to contribute positively to the community and develop a sense of social responsibility.

Enhanced Communication Skills:
Student leaders often need to communicate effectively with diverse groups of people. This experience can enhance their written and verbal communication skills.

Problem-Solving Skills:
Dealing with various issues and challenges within the student body or the university requires effective problem-solving skills. Student government positions provide opportunities to hone these skills.

Understanding Governance:
Serving in a student government role offers a practical understanding of governance structures, decision-making processes, and the dynamics of working within an organization.

Personal Satisfaction:
Many individuals find personal satisfaction in contributing to the improvement of campus life, representing their peers, and making a positive impact on the university community.

Election FAQs

Q: Are there grade requirements to be eligible to run?

A: Yes, all students must be in, and maintain, “good standing” with the University. Requirements vary amongst different colleges. In the College of Liberal Arts and Sciences (CLAS), a student must be not on either academic or disciplinary probation. CLAS requirements: 2.0 or higher, and not on disciplinary probation. Eligibility will be verified by the SEC, the Registrar, and the Office of Student Accountability.

Q: Where can I find the election materials online?

A: Elections information will be made available under the "Elections" tab of the main USG website. Campaign materials and forms will be made on the SEC Engage portal.

Q: In what ways can a campaign interact with student organization leaders before the campaigning period?

A: Prospective candidates are not allowed to contact heads of student organizations until after the approved candidate meeting. After campaigns have been verified at the meeting, they may contact heads of student orgs about meeting with them to secure support during the designated campaign period.

If you seek the support of a student org or business, you must fill out the appropriate paperwork available on Engage. Once the paperwork is approved by the SEC, you are allowed to claim support from a student org, but you are not allowed to claim support from a private business. Private business forms only allow for candidates to post campaign materials in the premises.

Reference: https://usg.uiowa.edu/get-involved/elections/election-faqs
